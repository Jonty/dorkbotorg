Isak Eldh kommer att visa och demonstrera egentillverkade musikinstrument. Senare under kvällen framför han en konsert på dessa.

Klubb #3. Simon Morris presented his project Musique Concrete and performed later on the same night.



Schedule
Dorbkot: 19.30
Klubb Dånk!: 21.00

Location: Röda Sten

Fee: Free entrance for the whole evening!

Presenter
Simon Morris (US / FR) is a new media artist who works with interactive instruments and new interfaces. His project Musique Concrete is an interactive performance piece which explores urban landscapes, skateboarding and sound. Mounted underneath the skateboard is a wireless device and sensors which enable the rider to modify sounds in realtime. The movements of the skateboarder determines the soundtrack of the city. The result is a musical composition which transforms the skateboarder into a composer. Investigating new forms of musical expression, Musique Concrete examines technology and its role as a socially engaged art practice. Simon has conducted live performances at Eyebeam, NYC, the Article Biennale 2006 in Stavanger, Norway, Lemurplex in NYC and the Kiasma Museum in Helsinki, Finland.

Collective @ Geiger Festival 2008 http://dorkbot.org/dorkbotgbg/?p=94 http://dorkbot.org/dorkbotgbg/?p=94#comments Sat, 11 Oct 2008 16:27:47 +0000 admin http://dorkbot.org/dorkbotgbg/?p=94 Dorkbot-GBG #3 took place on Saturday 11th October, in conjunction with GEIGER Festival 2008. The Institute for Transacoustic Research (IFTAF) from Vienna, and sound artist Mattias Petersson, each talked about their work and showed their custom-built live instruments, which they performed with later the same night. Dånk! Collective presented some of their low-tech sound projects for public space and audio experiments with bicycles.


(Picture: IFTAF)

Time: 18.00-19.30
Location: bEAT, Brew House, Åvägen 24, Göteborg
Fee: Free entrance as usual for Dorkbot, fee for accessing the rest of the festival

Presenters
IFTAF (Institute for Transacoustic Research) (AT) : The Institute for Transacoustic Research was founded in 1998 in Vienna to define and research transacoustics. Transacoustic research is concerned with the peripheral effects and tangential areas of acoustics, with their borders to other areas of research. Transacoustic research carries out science by means of art and art by means of science. The antiquated differentiation of these two areas is rejected and methods and settings from both areas are combined to arrive at unique lines of connection and division. IFTAF, which performs regularly at various electronic music festivals around the world with their custom-made musical instruments, will be represented here by Jörg Piringer (radio artist, sound poet, musician) and Ernst Reitermaier(experimental musician and radio art as well)

Mattias Petersson (SE) : Stockholm-based composer and sound artist Mattias Petersson’s music has been described as a mix between experimental electronica, electro-acoustic, industry and crackling noise. Since his debut album “Mimer” (Fylkingen Records, 2003), which was based upon sounds from a defunct iron mine, Petersson’s industrial soundscape has been mixed with clean waveforms, piano and orchestra samples, often treated through dirty filters and digital distortion. Mattias also released some music on his own label “Wickerwork Recordings”, and contributes with a track on the recently released MP3 compilation “The Post_piano2 Open Remix Project” on the label 12k. Besides working as solo artist he also collaborates with video artists and musician Fredrik Olofsson and many others, and plays his music around the world.

Dånk! Collective (SE) : Dånk! is a multidisciplinary collective based in Göteborg that works at the intersection of art, technology and design, with a particular focus on sound. The collective’s activities include art projects, HCI/Ubicomp research projects, organisation of events (Dorkbot, club nights, festivals, conferences, workshops), consulting in interaction design and new technologies, and freelance teaching in the context of seminars, participatory workshops, student supervision and more. Dånk! consists of Alexander Berman, Lalya Gaye, Olle Huge, Mappe Persson, Oscar Ramos, and Richard Widerberg.
