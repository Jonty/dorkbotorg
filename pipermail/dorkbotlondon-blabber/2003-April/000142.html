I'm interested in having a fiddle with this stuff. Printer guts I can't 
help you with (electronics and I have settled on a non-aggression pact 
after some nasty incidents involving electrocution), but the 
software/programming side I can probably give a shot at. What sort of 
thing are you looking to do with this?

I'd second the earlier comments about going for open-source drivers 
(Linux also springs to mind) first. HP are among the most 'open' 
printers to work with, and they make good printers. You might find the 
HP language that describes the pictures  (analogous to PostScript, but 
the name has slipped my mind for the moment) will have sufficient 
control. This would be really convenient, but a long-shot - these 
languages were specifically designed so people wouldn't have to fiddle 
with head positions, etc.

Depending on the result you want; you might find an old dot-matrix 
printer easier to work with. The technology is so old and so simple that 
it might give you more possibilities for hacking with.

Richard
