On Wed, 2 Apr 2003, Richard Cottrill wrote:

> Depending on the result you want; you might find an old dot-matrix 
> printer easier to work with. The technology is so old and so simple that 
> it might give you more possibilities for hacking with.

I have vague recollections of someone doing this kinda (art) work with an 
old dot matrix printer. This would involve writing directly to the serial 
(or parallel) port (I wouldn't know about USB). That part would be 
seriously fun to do, but the reverse engineering side (unless the 
printer's device driver's "open") would be hard work (waaay beyond my area 
of expertise).

OTOH, if you figured out the form of the serial port protocol, you should 
be able to do this without an OS (e.g. using a microcontroller or SBC).

BTW, anyone 'round here ever tried hacking USB? USB based microcontrollers 
are beginning to come on the market....
