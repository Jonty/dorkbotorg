On Sun, 13 Apr 2003, alex wrote:

> any ideas about the cheapest and easiest way of getting a high quality
> stereo feed to a roomful of people wearing headphones is much
> appreciated.

Disclaimer: This is off the top of my head...

OK, here's your spec (as far as I can see): You want to do this on the
cheap (i.e. you don't want to get together a 50 channel headphone
distribution amp). And you don't want to build 100 amplifiers (two per
headphone) -- the contraption having a total of 200 op-amps, hundreds of
resistors, caps, etc. So...

Why not get a hi power, stereo audio amplifier and run all 50 headphones
off it? You'd have to be careful about the load on the amplifier (some
combination of serial/parallel hookup should do, or some buffering of
the signal...). It would make your life much easier if all the headphone
had the same impedance (ideally same make/model). If you do this, probably
a good idea to have all the headphones permanently attached yo the system
(don't want to have audience members causing pops/cracks or blowing
fuses).

I haven't done the math, but seems like the simplest solution...
