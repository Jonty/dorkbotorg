Dear Cartographers,

The Cartographic Congress, a meeting of maps and mapmakers from all corners
of cartography begins its schedule of events this Sunday.

Starting on Sunday 11th with a field trip to Kennington Park, Collaborative
Mapping week launches with a Monday night Unix warm-up, then the
Collaborative Cartography show 'n tell on Tuesday 13th and the first
London-based game of noderunner on Thursday 15th May. Then, to round it all
off, a pirate party on Hijacker Island (Limehouse).

The map room and the hall of maps were inaugurated this Monday with the
installation of maps and cartographic materials
(maps/books/leaflets/texts/computers) which will be open regularly (but do
email/phone to make sure before dropping in). Please do bring more materials
of all kinds - there's plenty of pinboard space left, and its never too late
to come by with a project.

The Map Room
http://twenteenthcentury.com/uo/index.php/TheMapRoom

Please find full scheduling information for the next week below. See you at
the Congress!

cheers,

 the Cc

----------------- Monday 19th - Friday 23th May ---------
WEEK 2 THEME: Mapping Contemporary Capitalism
http://docs.metamute.org/view/Home/McC

Mapping Contemporary Capitalism (McC) is a long term software
development project with the goal of creating a tool for mapping
relations of power. The software is based on newly emerging
opensource protocols for data management and visualisation and
borrows much of its knowledge (The Semantic Web) from Internet
pioneers such as Tim Berners-Lee and cartographers such as Bureau
d'Etudes.

As part of the CartographicCongress we are planning to run a research
week dedicated to the project 'Mapping Contemporary Capitalism' from
the 19 May until the 23 May. The plan is to bring together people who
have a long term interest in working on the project so as to work on
a preliminary research framework. At the end of the week we would
look to have agreed on working practice, research areas and
prototyping strategies (all of this we would look to have distilled
into a report by the end of the week).

Technologies:
McC will be based on the following open protocols:

* RDF - Resource Description Framework
* RSS - Rich Site Summary
* FOAF - Friend Of A Friend
* OWL - Web Ontology Language
* SVG - Scalable Vector Graphics

recognised as having great nature conservation importance. However,

But many other sites - Bentwaters Parks and Woodbridge USAF/RAF
