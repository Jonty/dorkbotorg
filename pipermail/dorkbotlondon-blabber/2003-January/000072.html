next meeting:
wednesday, january 8th, 2003 at 7pm at the Columbia University Computer 
Music Center (directions and info at: http://dorkbot.org)


featuring the lovely and talented:

Cory Arcangel will present recent work revolving around the 6502 
microprocessor. Subjects most likely will include acid house, flavor 
flav, & late 80's commodore 64 video graffiti.

Cory Arcangel is an artist living in manhattan. He is part of the beige 
programming crew aka BEIGE, and the Radical Software Group.

Dafna Naphtali will talk about her Max/MSP programs for sound processing 
of voice and other instruments that she's been creating and using since 
'93 -- among them an elaborate and sprawling software control patch for 
the Eventide H3000 effects processor for her {kaleid-o-phone} projects 
-- and her work at Engine 27 (www.engine27.org), and What is it Like to 
be a Bat? (www.whatbat.org).

Liubo Borissov will present recent work exploring video synthesis from 
sound and talk about some of the tools available for representing and 
reinterpreting sound as visual data.

Liubo is a graduate student in physics at Columbia University.
