program december 28

txOom by f0.am at http://www.f0.am/txoom/
txOom is an ephemeral zone in which pliant, exobiological media worlds 
coagulate into physical existence, absorbing their dwellers in a 
softened, wrinkled reality. The participants are invited to actively 
take part in the environment's growth and transformation, thereby 
shaping their visual, sonic and tactile experience of this embodied 
space stretching between the physical and the digital.
  	The name, txOom ('wrinkled' in hmong dialect of Laos) is an amalgam 
between 'textile' and 'bloom', two terms guiding the aesthetic and the 
functional research of the project. The txOom spaces are as flexible, 
malleable and interconnected as fibres in a textile, with an ability to 
grow, evolve and unfold like plants in bloom. The outer shells are 
existing, physical spaces that situate txOom in particular local 
contexts, providing a fertile social and cultural ground from which to 
sprout. These shells form a protective layer for two malleable skins of 
the environment; the soft architectures made of supple textiles and the 
responsive synthetic media worlds, and the visitors; their bodies in 
motion, their spatial navigation and patterns of gestures weave 
themselves into the fabric of the environment, becoming the most 
fragile, humid and alive layer in it. Their experiences feed the 
emergence of txOom, that consumes their behaviours and transforms them 
into immersive audiovisual
   environments.
  	txOom has been developed as series of public experiments, which move 
away from the static representations of art and culture, and towards 
environments where the public becomes an essential part of the work, 
where the synaesthesia of the forms involved creates a coherent tangible 
experience.

The makers of txOom come and talk about their work.
