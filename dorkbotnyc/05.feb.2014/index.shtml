It featured the subsong syllable sequences of:

Mark Skwarek: Play AR
Play AR [augmented reality] is the world's easiest augmented reality video game creation software in a mobile app. Play AR takes video games out of the TV and turns them into a physical experience! Play AR was designed to let people with no programming experience play and create highly interactive augmented reality [AR] video games with just a few clicks. Best of all you can publish your creations as stand alone apps for smart phones and tablets!
http://www.markskwarek.com


Brad Garton & Damon Holzborn: RTcmix
Brad Garton and Damon Holzborn will be discussing the latest capabilities of RTcmix, especially in its 'embedded' form working on iOS and Android devices. RTcmix is a multi-platform music and audio processing system with extended algorithmic and DSP capabilities. Garton and Holzborn will also be demonstrating several projects that showcase the ease with which audio/music processes can be developed using RTcmix.
http://rtcmix.org


Masashi Nakatani: TECHTILE toolkit
TECHTILE toolkit is a device with which people can prototype touch experiences. This system enables the recording, editing, and playback of feelings of touch in digital format, and sharing via the internet. As a collaborative project with Yamaguchi Center for Arts and Media (YCAM), we've hosted workshops that enable people to realize and play with touch modality at universities and art/science museums. Working progress of this project, including an interactive demo, is presented in this talk. Collaborators: Yasuaki Kakehi, Kouta Minamizawa, Soichiro Mihara, Susumu Tachi.
http://www.techtile.org/en
