Dorkbot.detroit 01
08 Dec 2004

The first Dorkbot.detroit session was held live on the air and over the internet via 88.3 FM WCBN Ann Arbor. A panel consisting of Interactive Technologies radio program host Pan, experimental musician and event promoter Adam Mokan, artist and experimental musican Jeff Karolski, and programmer/musician Joe Bauer, were present to interview and discuss the method, equipment used, and the general backgrounds of three electronic musicians:

 

Will Soderberg
audio/video artist/producer/writer based in southfield, michigan...
I will be using a kaoss pad to control & process software synth on my laptop...could maybe be described as "abstract experimental electronic music with an underlying feel of futuristic psychedelia..." recorded 1st electronic composition project (the-one-that-runs-through) at wmu electronic music lab in 1985...

Listen to Will's performance.

level-4
soundist / designer
Mixing bliphop beats, found and constructed sounds into evolving landscapes. "i think of sound and technology as a collaborative partners in my work." -scott brennen aka level-4

Listen to Level-4's performance.

bubblegone
I intend to create audio as if it were memory through exploring digital and analogue improvisational techniques. Our memories are samples of our life. When these samples are played in our minds they loop, mix, layer together, and fade away into other thoughts or into nothing. My music emulates this process of discovery and the search for one's self.

Listen to bubblegone's performance.

 

The bond for bringing these artists together lay in the use of improvisational methods and the search for experimental tactile manipulation of audio, as a opposed to sequencing and linear arrangment. By making use of equipment like the Korg Kaoss pad and equipment with touch pads, the linear quality has been removed, and the artist is free to roam the sound spectrum. The end result is different for each artist ranging from the futuristic psychedelia noise of Will S., evoloving beats of level-4, and the found sound and broken melodies of bubblegone.
