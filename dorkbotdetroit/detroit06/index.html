Dorkbot.detroit 06
9 March 2006 • 7pm-9pm 

Dorkbot.detroit at Sync 2006

The 6th dorkbot.detroit took place at the University of Michigan's Duderstat Center as apart of the Digital Arts Festival, Sync. We opened the performance portion of the festival by kicking off the first of it's three days. This time around we had three very good presenters who brought their own unique style of the audio arts to present to the audience a diverse look the electronic music and electronic story telling.

There were two common bounds between these presenters:

1) As far as electronics were concerned - we had one low tech, one high tech, and one in the middle.

2) Each told a story.

The first presenter, Brian Schorn, performed "an episode of little consequence." This work was written as a narrative which incorporated manipulated vocals and a dynamic sonic arrangement. His sounds originated from rocks, a flute, and an assortment of digital manipulated sound samples.

Jeff Karolski performed an intimate piece that let the listener share a conversation with his grandmother while he was suffering with a cold. Folowing the conversation, our Jeff took a contemplative moment which was expressed in the amplification of a ringing wine glass before going back to see his grandmother to complete the task he came there to do, fix her hearing aid.

Chris Landau the audience decide how to navigate the web based science fiction story, The Flocking Party. Laser pointers were distributed through out the audience who then used them to point to what they were reading or looking at. Chris would then click on the interactive elements were ever the main body of the "flock" moved to. The story itself took place in the future and audio portion of the story was reactive to where the reader's mouse was on the screen.

Below are the bios for each performer:

Brian Schorn:

Interdisciplinary artist, Brian Schorn, composes and performs experimental electronic, computer and electro-acoustic music while integrating video, performance art, installation art and poetry.

Schorn received an MFA in Electronic Music and Recording Media at Mills College in Oakland, California. He studied composition with Fred Frith, Pauline Oliveros and Alvin Curran.

His sounds are generated from various means including digital and analog synthesis, digital and analog processing, field recording, home-made electronic circuits, acoustic world instruments, custom-built instruments, spoken word and vocal exploration.

http://www.circulus.us

 

Jeff Karolski:

Jeff Karolski is a Detroit-based artist who although schooled for visual arts has found himself applying his sculpting sensibilities within time based mediums. With a foundation paved by a personal tape recorder, today Jeff has been realizing his work through more temporary experiences such as performance and/or interactive processes in a variety of different contexts.

Notable works: opening the first DEMF "playing" a model-builder's table saw along with a fly trapped inside his bathroom, a 10 year collection of answering machine message tapes found at thrift stores installed at DUMBO Art Space in Brooklyn, a commissioned outdoor performance at the DIA mid-winter where Jeff took off his shirt and shoes to conduct the cold resulting in the amplification of his teeth chattering on a microphone element while cars driving by triggered a space heater, and a performance marching through NYC's Central Park with a Hypersonic Speaker projecting the sound of a parade at park-goers. (The sound from this speaker projects like light from a flashlight, so only the person who it's being pointed at can hear it.)

http://www.woodcheese.com

 

Chris Landau:

Chris Landau is currently finishing his MFA thesis at the University of Michigan, School of Art and Design, and he received his BFA in Printmaking from the Cleveland Institute of Art. Chris’s creative work ranges from traditional to digital media in projects that bridge scientific visualization and fine art. His current written work is a synthesis of both media theory and cognitive evolution.

Chris’s most recent project, theflockingparty.com, brings many of his favorite themes together, living systems, multi-linear narrative, and speculative technologies. He has also begun illustrating a cognitive psychology book by the esteemed psychologist, Steven Kaplan. In addition, he will be creating an animation of yeast cell development for Organelle View, a project that visualizes the protein database created by Kumar Lab at the University of Michigan. Upon graduation, Chris plans on moving to Philadelphia to be with his wife and start a company that feeds his interest in the life sciences and digital media through inventive forms of visualization.

http://www.theflockingparty.co
