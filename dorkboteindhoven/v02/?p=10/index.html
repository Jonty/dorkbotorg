DORKBOT #3 - review
21 September 2005, Gaslab, TU/e

After a lengthy summer vacation DorkbotEindhoven returned to the Gaslab on September 21st. As at our 2nd dorkbot Maarten Pieterson of the Studium Generale moderated once again making sure that presentations didn’t go overtime and asking interesting questions of the presenters. Though some last minute changes had to be made to our program the first DorkbotEindhoven of the year started off well with a lengthy and interesting program.

The first presenter of the night was Wilfried Hou Je Bek. Wilfried Hou Je Bek left school at 16 to become an astronaut but this turned out to be bad careermove and he founded socialfiction.org instead. Socialfiction.org is a website (obviously) dealing with psychogeography, cartography, diy urbanism, as well as the organising party behind generative psychogeographical walks, events and presentations. Right now socialfiction.org is busy running The Crystalpunk Workshop for Soft Architecture. This workshop, which will take place over a period of 3 months will evolve an empty room from nothingness into unknown states of technological enhancement. Unlike the alphabet that always knows where it is going, this workshop does not. It has an open door policy and everybody is welcomed to participate and will culminate at the Impakt Festival, taking place at the end of December in Utrecht. Wilfried presented the workshop speaking mostly about the concepts of crystals and punk, the way in which they evolve organically and create entirely new and unexpected forms. www.socialfiction.org / www.socialfiction.org/crystalpunk

Next up we were lucky to have a presentation from Enrike Hurtado Mendieta and Thor Magnusson of IXI software. They were close to ending their residency at the STEIM in Amsterdam and had shown their interest in making a presentation at DorkbotEindhoven, which was happy to host them. Enrike Hurtado Mendieta and Thor Magnusson are musicians and programmers who founded ixi software in year 2000. Ixi software is a collective of people (including Enrike and Thor) interested in the creation of musical instruments. Their focus is on screen based graphical user interfaces for open source audio programming languages. Both
physical and virtual interfaces are of interest and on the website there are 15+ applications that can be downloaded for free. Ixi talked about the philosophy behind their work, being open-source, showed some of the Instruments available in the software, explained the history of ixi and demoed some of the features of ixi as well as some short pieces produced with it. www.ixi-software.net

The third presentation of the night was by Alexander Rulkens. Alexander Rulkens is a 4th year student in Industrial Design at the TU/eindhoven. His focus is constantly searching for answers to questions like: What is the purpose of design? Why do humans have the urge to design the world around them? Why are there so many bad designs? What makes something a good design anyway? The project he presented was a Luminaire built to allow a user to play with light. Each movement the user makes in relation to the Luminaire directly influenced the light emanating from the object. One could pull, push, stretch, launch, dim or create any light one might want. Alexander took us through the process of building the Luminaire, talking first about the concept of lights, shapes of light, user interfaces and interaction and later showing us his preliminary sketches of the Luminaire, as well as talking about the actual process of building the physical object. Towards the end of the discussion he talked of his interest of somehow allowing the light made by the Luminare to interact with sound by connecting the object to a computer running a piece of software such as ixi.

Last, but not least came the last-minute addition of the night, a presentation by Gijs Gieskes. Gijs Gieskes is an Eindhoven-based electronic-musician, circuit-bender and instrument-maker. At DorkbotEindhoven he talked a little about his work in circuit-bending and object-modification in order to turn those objects into instruments. He spent most of his presentation showing a variety of ‘instruments’ such as a modified tape-recorder, a tonal-wheel, a gameboy, as well as sharing with us some diy-sketches of diy-circuits. He also demoed each of the instruments so the audience could get an idea of what they sounded like and invited the audience on stage to see each of the objects up-close. www.gieskes.nl / experimentalmusicalinstruments.gieskes.nl
