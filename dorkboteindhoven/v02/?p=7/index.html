DORKBOT#1

DORKBOT #1 - review
6 April 2005, Gaslab, TU/e

On April 6th DORKBOT EINDHOVEN launched its first evening at the Gaslab,TU/eindhoven. Dorkbot Eindhoven is part of a global movement of small, autonomous functioning organizations with a similar goal: offering artists/programmers/engineers/geeks an opportunity for informal peer review; establish a forum for the presentation of new art works/technology/software/hardware; help establish relationships and foster collaboration between people with various backgrounds and interests. It is the initiative of Art & Technology Festival STRP in collaboration with Studium Generale (TU/e).

The first Eindhoven Dorkbot showcased a variety of projects and artists doing work with media, technology and code. Participating artists were: Koert van Mensvoort, NMN/dorkbot ghent, Danielle Roberts and Digital Art Group Maastricht. Each of these artists had a different take on using technology to present ideas related to art, aesthetics, society, or just allowing their imagination to let loose. Like any evening people poured in slowly at the beginning, but the event was quite popular with around 50 guests in the audience.

After a short introduction about Dorkbot and Dorkbot Eindhoven from our moderator Ruud, NMN/Dorkbot Ghent kicked off the first Eindhoven Dorkbot with their presentation. First an introduction of Dorkbot Ghent was made including its 4 year history and the vision it tries to bring to each dorkbot they organize and a second component based on the projects of NMN. NMN is a 5 year old non-profit organization that started Dorkbot Ghent, and also functions as an artist group that has been producing a variety of digital art projects involving sound and/or interactivity. Some of the projects shown involved either action script or processing code and the creation of various dynamic visuals based on web-cam feed or code-coordinates. http://www.nmn.be

Like any evening involving technology there were a few problems, fortunately for us solved quite quickly so the second presenter of the night was Koert Van Mensvoert. He is both a multimedia artist and a scientist and is currently acting assistant professor in the Industrial Design Department of the TU/e. With an upbeat, ironic presentation Koert showed a variety of works: Amateurs - a short film where 2 men play-fight with special effects; Bos ruikt naar Shampoo - a fragment of a film about how living in today’s media saturated society affects the way we view and analyze nature, especially in the case of children; Datafountain - a short amusing film about a project which revolved around the idea of building a water fountain where one could see the difference in the value of the yen, euro and the dollar in a visual way; Drift - short internet dance film about a project related to motion capture techniques; and lastly Active Cursor - an interactive cursour program that allows the cursor to have its own life rather than have to be under constant user control.
http://www.koert.com

The third presenter of the night was webartist, Danielle Roberts. Originally trained as a sculptor and monument designer she has been active in new media since 1998. Her works most often use photography, scans, video, and text combined with programming and focus on the fusion of 3 equal components: the computer, the visitor and the artist, which all interact to become part of the whole. The project she presented, NuMuseum is a compilation of all of the works she has been creating on the web in the last years, allowing visitors to search through php-coded archives as well as creating entirely new works based on recycled web materials.
http://www.numuseum.nl

The first dorkbot ended with the presentation by three members of the Digital Art Group Maastricht. Berto Aussems, a self-taught new media artist working in the field since 1981, talked about the history of digital media in the netherlands and especially the Maastricht area, the founding of the Electronic Art Collective Limburg in the early ’90s and subsequent foundation of the Digital Art Group Maastricht in 1999. He presented his own work Peristaltique which animates randomized graphics based on motion algorithms. Visual artist Andre Dezelak showed Morface, a morphing-animation film he made by choosing a variety of portraits he had painted in the 80’s and then morphing them into each other. Multimedia artist Rene Terhorst generally focuses on user interfaces that use brainwaves as input and create a virtual reality world as output. He screened a few recordings of animations that were controlled via brainwaves or that reacted to different types of brainwaves.
http://www.digitalartgroup.nl
