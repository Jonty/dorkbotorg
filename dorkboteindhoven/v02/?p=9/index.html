DORKBOT #4 - review
30 November 2005, Gaslab, TU/e

November 30th brought us to our 4th DorkbotEindhoven, this time moderated by Vivian vdG. We had planned to launch OpenDork for the first time alongside there project presentations, but as usual things didn’t quite turn out as expected. At the last moment one of the presenters got sick, and we were left with two presentations and an OpenDork which might not work out. However, as always, dorkbot was full of surprises and the night was wonderful.

We started off with a presentation by Gabriel (a.k.a “Salsaman”). Gabriel is a video artist, VJ, Free Software programmer and the main developer of LiVES (available for download at http://lives.sourceforge.net), a Free, Open Source video editing application and VJ tool. He is also one of the main people involved with designing livido (check out http://www.livido.org) a new standard for video processing plugins, which will soon be ready for release. At dorkbot Salsaman gave a demo of LiVES showing the main features of the software as well as some which are still in developemtn. A mini-workshop in LiVES and a little VJing for those in the audience. He also showed the new multitrack feature which is still under development and some of the video editing and effects tools available in the software. A few conversations started up around LiVES features and future developments, especially in relation to linking LiVES to audio tools. After the presentation a piece produced with LiVES was screened.

The second presentation of the night was of some of the light objects of Jos Kranen (http://www.joskranen.nl) who presented his work alongside a member of of Metatronics. Jos Kranen (Tegelen, 1977) graduated from the Design Academy Eindhoven in December 2004 with his project “flexilight” a flexible lighting system that consists of loose parts, so that the user can define his or her own electricity network throughout the living space. Through this project Jos wanted to combine his passion for design with his affection for electronics and also expand on his belief that the wonderful electronics invented worldwide need a bridge to the user. At dorkbot he presented both “flexilight” and a recent project “vase light.” “Vase light,” a project developed in cooperation with Frederik Molenschot (Breda, 1981, also a Design Academy graduate), was custom made for the Dutch Design Week 2005. In this case the light object uses water as its conductor and is meant to be placed in a regular vase filled with water and flowers. (Like the DIYers that we are at dorkbot we cut empty coke bottles and filled them with water.) The Vase Light provides the same amount of light we find in an ordinary 15 watt light bulb, but uses a power LED as light source. The technical part of the project was produced by Metatronics, a small company located on the TUE site. www.joskranen.nl / www.frederikmolenschot.nl / www.metatronics.nl

To end the night we launched OpenDork. The OpenDork concept is the dorkbot equivalent of an open-mic, which has been tried and tested at other dorkbots around the world because it offers random members of the audience or any dorks with smaller projects to present their works. The first presentation of OpenDorkEindhoven was made by poeticterror .com with a little assistance from one of the main dorkboteindhoven organizers who presented a few visualizations of math algorithms which he coded in flash action script.
