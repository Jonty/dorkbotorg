16 January 2008

Location: Marian Koshland Science Museum

DorkbotDC + Make:DC Inaugural Joint Workshop!  :  We built an LED Cube based on one of Make: Blog's Weekend Projects

This was the first-ever joint meeting of Dorkbot DC and Make:DC, and the first-ever meeting of Make:DC!

During this meeting, we built LED cubes based on on the one from the Make blog but we're improved on it by using an open-source Arduino microcontroller instead of an ATtiny2313V.

Participants brought as many of the listed items as they could:

    TOOLS:
  • soldering iron and stand
  • solder
  • wire cutters*
  • wire strippers
  • needle nose pliers
  • insulating tape (black electrical tape works well) and/or shrink-fit tubing
    PARTS:
  • Arduino or compatible unit
    • Diecimila PLUS USB board (fully assembled)
    • Boarduino (solderless Arduino clone)
    • Bare Bones Arduino Kit (unassembled)
      Please have your Arduino assembled by the meeting, if you buy it unassembled.
    • Arduinos and other related components.
  • 47 Ohm resistor (yellow purple brown striped)
  • 27 LEDs
  • Wire (or paper clips work pretty well for this type of application)
  • Case for it all to go in. (you might be able to repurpose something as a case for more of a 'prototype' look. E.g., the Boarduino and a 9V battery should fit in a big Altoids case.)
  • 9VDC @ min 500mA power supply (e.g., wall-wart transformer)

Some kits were pre-ordered at a discount.

Experts were on-hand to help novices with soldering techniques and other instruction. Those under 18 had to have full-time supervision by at least one legal guardian. [Photo from Make: Blog]
